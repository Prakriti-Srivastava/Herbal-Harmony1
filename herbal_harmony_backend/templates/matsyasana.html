MATSYASANA (FISH POSE)

Matsyasana opens the chest, throat, and abdomen while improving posture and strengthening the upper back. It enhances lung capacity, stimulates the throat chakra, and relieves fatigue and tension in the shoulders and neck.

Sanskrit Meaning

Matsya = Fish

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Fish Pose”

Steps to Perform Matsyasana:

  1. Lie down flat on your back with legs together and hands beside your body.
  2. Slide your palms under your hips, keeping elbows close to your body.
  3. Inhale and gently lift your chest upward while arching the back.
  4. Drop your head backward so the crown touches the floor gently.
  5. Keep the weight on your elbows — not on your head.
  6. Hold the pose while breathing normally.
  7. Exhale, lower the chest, bring the head up, and relax.

Benefits of Matsyasana:

  • Opens chest and lungs for better breathing
  • Improves posture and spinal flexibility
  • Reduces neck and shoulder tension
  • Stimulates thyroid and throat region
  • Relieves mild back pain and fatigue
  • Enhances digestion and metabolic function

Precautions

Avoid if you have severe neck, back, or spinal injury.

Do not put pressure on the head; support the weight with elbows.

People with high or low blood pressure should avoid.

Avoid during pregnancy.
