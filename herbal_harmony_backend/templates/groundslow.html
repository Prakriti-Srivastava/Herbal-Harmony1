GROUNDING AND SLOW BREATHING

Grounding and Slow Breathing is a calming mental wellness practice that may help reduce stress, anxiety, tension, and mental overload. Gentle breathing and grounding techniques support emotional calmness and relaxation.

Mental Wellness Practice

Stress Relief and Grounding Support

Related practices: Slow Breathing, Grounding Exercise, Calm Sitting

Steps to Perform Grounding and Slow Breathing:

  1. Sit comfortably in a quiet place with your back relaxed.
  2. Place your feet gently on the floor and relax your shoulders.
  3. Close your eyes and slowly inhale through your nose.
  4. Exhale gently and make the exhale slightly longer than the inhale.
  5. Focus on your breathing and the feeling of your body touching the ground.
  6. Continue slow breathing for 5–10 minutes.
  7. Take short calming breaks from screens and loud environments.

Benefits of Grounding and Slow Breathing:

  • Helps calm the mind and body.
  • May reduce stress and anxiety.
  • Supports emotional balance and relaxation.
  • Promotes better breathing awareness.
  • May improve focus and mental comfort.

Beginner Tips

Keep your breathing natural and comfortable.

Avoid forcing deep breaths if it feels uncomfortable.

Short calming breaks during the day may help reduce mental overload.

Better sleep and reduced screen time may improve emotional wellness.
