ARDHA MATSYENDRASANA
(HALF LORD OF THE FISHES POSE)

Ardha Matsyendrasana is a gentle spinal twisting pose that improves flexibility, detoxifies the body, and enhances digestion. It stimulates the spinal nerves, opens the chest and shoulders, and promotes better posture and relaxation.

Sanskrit Meaning

Ardha = Half

Matsyendra = Lord of the Fishes (a yogi)

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Half Lord of the Fishes Pose”

Steps to Perform Ardha Matsyendrasana:

  1. Sit on the floor with legs extended in front of you.
  2. Bend your right knee and place the right foot outside the left thigh.
  3. Fold your left leg and place the foot near the right hip.
  4. Inhale, lengthen your spine upward.
  5. Exhale, twist your torso to the right and place your left elbow on the right knee.
  6. Place your right hand behind your back for support.
  7. Look over your right shoulder and maintain the twist gently.
  8. Hold the pose for 20–30 seconds, breathing deeply.
  9. Release and repeat on the other side.

Benefits of Ardha Matsyendrasana:

  • Improves spinal flexibility and posture
  • Enhances digestion and detoxifies organs
  • Stimulates liver and kidneys
  • Reduces back pain and stiffness
  • Strengthens shoulders, neck, and hips
  • Improves energy flow in the body

Beginner Tips

Do not force the twist; rotate only as much as comfortable.

Keep the spine straight throughout the pose.

Use a folded blanket under the hips if flexibility is low.

Breathe deeply to ease into the twist.
