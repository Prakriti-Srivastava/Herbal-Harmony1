PADMASANA (LOTUS POSE)

Padmasana is a classical seated yoga posture that promotes deep meditation, calmness of mind, and flexibility of the lower body. It stabilizes the spine and balances energy flow throughout the body.

Sanskrit

Padmasana (pud-MAH-sah-nah)

padma = lotus

asana = posture

Meaning: “Lotus Pose”

Steps to Perform Padmasana:

  1. Sit on the floor with your legs stretched out in front of you.
  2. Bend your right knee and place your right foot on your left thigh.
  3. Now bend your left knee and place your left foot on your right thigh.
  4. Keep your spine straight and rest your hands on your knees in Chin mudra or Gyan mudra.
  5. Close your eyes and breathe slowly, sitting calmly for 1–5 minutes.

Benefits of Padmasana:

  • Calms the mind and reduces stress and anxiety.
  • Improves posture and strengthens the spine.
  • Enhances hip, knee, and ankle flexibility.
  • Promotes better digestion and activates abdominal organs.
  • Ideal for meditation and pranayama practice.

Beginner Tips

If Padmasana feels too intense, start with Ardha Padmasana (Half Lotus).

Warm up your hips with butterfly pose (Baddha Konasana) before practicing.

Avoid forcing your legs into position to prevent knee injury.

Use a cushion or folded blanket to elevate your hips for comfort.
