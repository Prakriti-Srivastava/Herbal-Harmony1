SHASHANKASANA (CHILD POSE)

Shashankasana is a calming forward–bending posture that helps reduce stress, relaxes the spine, and brings emotional grounding. This pose releases tension from the back, shoulders, and mind, making it ideal for relaxation and deep breathing.

Sanskrit Meaning

Shashank = Rabbit / Hare

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Rabbit/Child Pose”

Steps to Perform Shashankasana:

  1. Sit in Vajrasana with your spine straight and hands on your thighs.
  2. Inhale deeply and raise both arms straight above your head.
  3. Exhale slowly and bend forward from the waist.
  4. Stretch your arms forward and place your forehead gently on the floor.
  5. Relax your shoulders, back, and neck completely.
  6. Breathe slowly and stay in this pose for 20–40 seconds.
  7. Inhale, lift your torso up, and return to the starting position.

Benefits of Shashankasana:

  • Reduces stress, anxiety, and mental tension
  • Relaxes the back, shoulders, and neck muscles
  • Improves blood circulation to the brain
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Helps in emotional balance and grounding
  • Improves breathing by releasing chest tightness

Beginner Tips

Spread your knees slightly apart if you feel pressure in the abdomen.

Keep your arms stretched for deeper relaxation.

Do not force your head to touch the floor; go only as far as comfortable.

Practice in a peaceful environment for maximum calmness.
