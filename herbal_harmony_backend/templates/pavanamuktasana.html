PAVANAMUKTASANA
(WIND-RELIEVING POSE)

Pavanamuktasana is a calming reclined yoga pose that improves digestion, reduces gas and bloating, and releases tension from the lower back. It enhances abdominal functioning and promotes relaxation and circulation.

Sanskrit Meaning

Pavana = Air / Wind

Mukta = Release

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Wind-Relieving Pose”

Steps to Perform Pavanamuktasana:

  1. Lie down on your back with legs stretched out and arms by your sides.
  2. Bend your right knee and bring it toward your chest.
  3. Interlock your fingers over the shin or hold the knee.
  4. Exhale and gently pull the knee closer to your chest.
  5. Keep your left leg straight and your back flat on the floor.
  6. Hold the pose for 20–30 seconds while breathing normally.
  7. Release and repeat with the left leg.
  8. Finally, bend both knees to the chest for the full pose and hug them gently.
  9. Slowly release legs and relax.

Benefits of Pavanamuktasana:

  • Relieves gas, acidity, and bloating
  • Improves digestion and stimulates digestive organs
  • Reduces constipation
  • Releases stiffness in lower back and hips
  • Improves blood circulation in abdominal region
  • Calms the mind and reduces fatigue

Beginner Tips

Keep your spine fully relaxed on the mat.

Do not raise your head unless comfortable.

Avoid pressing the knee too hard into the chest.

Breathe deeply and maintain slow movements.
