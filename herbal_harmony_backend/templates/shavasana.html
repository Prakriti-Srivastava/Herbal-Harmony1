SHAVASANA (CORPSE POSE)

Shavasana is a deep relaxation posture practiced at the end of yoga sessions. It calms the mind, relaxes every muscle, reduces stress, and restores energy. This pose helps in grounding the body and improving overall mental clarity.

Sanskrit Meaning

Shava = Corpse

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Corpse Pose”

Steps to Perform Shavasana:

  1. Lie down flat on your back with legs slightly apart.
  2. Place your arms comfortably at your sides with palms facing upward.
  3. Close your eyes and take slow, deep breaths.
  4. Relax your toes, legs, hips, stomach, chest, shoulders, and face.
  5. Let your body feel heavy and completely relaxed.
  6. Stay in this pose for 5–10 minutes or until your body feels calm.
  7. To come out, gently wiggle your fingers and toes, turn to your right side, and sit up slowly.

Benefits of Shavasana:

  • Reduces stress, anxiety, and mental fatigue
  • Promotes full-body relaxation
  • Improves concentration and mental clarity
  • Normalizes heart rate and breathing
  • Helps in muscle recovery after yoga practice
  • Improves sleep quality

Beginner Tips

Keep your body completely still for best relaxation.

Do not control your breathing—let it happen naturally.

Use a thin blanket under the head or knees if needed for comfort.

Practice in a quiet environment for deeper relaxation.
