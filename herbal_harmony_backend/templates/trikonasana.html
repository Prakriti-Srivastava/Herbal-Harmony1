TRIKONASANA (TRIANGLE POSE)

Trikonasana is a standing yoga pose that improves flexibility, strengthens the legs, and opens the chest and shoulders. It enhances balance, stimulates digestion, and helps reduce stress while improving overall posture and body alignment.

Sanskrit Meaning

Trikona = Triangle

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Triangle Pose”

Steps to Perform Trikonasana:

  1. Stand with your feet 3–4 feet apart.
  2. Turn your right foot 90° outward and left foot slightly inward.
  3. Raise both arms parallel to the floor, palms facing down.
  4. Inhale and stretch your torso to the right side.
  5. Exhale and bend sideways from the waist, keeping your spine straight.
  6. Place your right hand on your ankle, shin, or the floor (as comfortable).
  7. Extend your left arm straight upward toward the ceiling.
  8. Turn your head to gaze at your left hand.
  9. Hold for 15–30 seconds, breathing normally.
  10. Come up slowly and repeat on the other side.

Benefits of Trikonasana:

  • Improves spine and leg flexibility
  • Strengthens thighs, knees, and ankles
  • Opens chest and shoulders
  • Improves digestion and boosts metabolism
  • Reduces back stiffness and body tension
  • Enhances balance and stability

Beginner Tips

Do not lean forward — keep your body in one plane.

Use a yoga block if your hand doesn’t reach the floor.

Keep knees slightly soft if you feel pressure.

Engage your core to maintain balance and stability.
