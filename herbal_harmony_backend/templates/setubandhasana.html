SETU BANDHASANA (BRIDGE POSE)

Setu Bandhasana, or Bridge Pose, is a backbend yoga posture that strengthens the spine, opens the chest, and stimulates the abdominal organs. It promotes flexibility and balance in the body while calming the mind.

Sanskrit

Setu Bandha Sarvangasana (SET-too BUHN-dah Sahr-von-GAH-sah-nah)

Setu = bridge

Bandha = lock

Sarva = all

Anga = limb

Steps to Perform Setu Bandhasana:

  1. Lie flat on your back with your knees bent and feet hip-width apart.
  2. Place your arms alongside your body, palms facing down.
  3. Press your feet and arms firmly into the mat and lift your hips upward while inhaling.
  4. Keep your thighs and feet parallel and engage your glutes and thighs.
  5. Hold the pose for a few breaths, then exhale slowly and lower your back down to the mat.

Benefits of Setu Bandhasana:

  • Strengthens the back, glutes, and legs.
  • Opens the chest and improves lung capacity.
  • Reduces anxiety, fatigue, and mild depression.
  • Improves digestion and stimulates abdominal organs.
  • Relieves tension in the neck and spine.

Beginner Tips

If you have tight shoulders or feel intense discomfort when you try to bind your hands behind your back, simply keep your arms alongside your body, palms down.

Maintaining the natural curve of your neck is a crucial element of the pose. Don’t press the back of your neck into the mat.

If you clasp your hands, broaden through your chest and shimmy your upper arms underneath your shoulders. Don’t forcefully draw your shoulders away from your ears, which can overstretch your neck.

Do not turn your head to one side while you’re in this pose. Keep your gaze straight toward the ceiling.
