USTRASANA (CAMEL POSE)

Ustrasana is a deep backbend that stretches the entire front body and strengthens the back muscles. It opens the chest, improves posture, and boosts energy levels. This pose also helps relieve fatigue, improves flexibility, and enhances lung capacity.

Sanskrit Meaning

Ustra = Camel

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Camel Pose”

Steps to Perform Ustrasana:

  1. Kneel on the floor with your knees hip-width apart.
  2. Place your hands on your hips and keep your spine straight.
  3. Inhale and lift your chest upward.
  4. Exhale and slowly bend backward, keeping the neck neutral.
  5. Reach your hands back and hold your heels one by one.
  6. Push your hips forward while lifting your chest up and opening your shoulders.
  7. Hold the position for 10–20 seconds, breathing slowly.
  8. To come out, bring hands back to hips and slowly rise up.

Benefits of Ustrasana:

  • Improves spinal flexibility
  • Strengthens back, shoulders, and arms
  • Opens the chest and lungs, improving breathing
  • Reduces neck and back stiffness
  • Boosts energy and reduces fatigue
  • Improves posture and tones the abdomen

Beginner Tips

Keep your thighs perpendicular to the floor for proper alignment.

If reaching your heels is hard, keep hands on lower back for support.

Do not drop your head too far back—keep neck relaxed.

Engage your core to protect the lower back.
