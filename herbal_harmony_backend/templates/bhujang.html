BHUJANGASANA (COBRA POSE)

Bhujangasana is a gentle backbend that strengthens the spine, opens the chest, and improves flexibility. It energizes the body, improves posture, and reduces stress and fatigue. It is an important part of Surya Namaskar.

Sanskrit Meaning

Bhujanga = Cobra / Serpent

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Cobra Pose”

Steps to Perform Bhujangasana:

  1. Lie down on your stomach with legs extended and feet slightly apart.
  2. Place your palms under your shoulders with elbows close to the body.
  3. Inhale and slowly lift your head and chest off the floor.
  4. Press your pelvis and legs firmly into the floor.
  5. Lift only till your navel area, keeping shoulders relaxed and away from ears.
  6. Hold the pose for 10–20 seconds while breathing normally.
  7. Exhale and gently lower your chest and forehead back to the floor.

Benefits of Bhujangasana:

  • Strengthens the spine and back muscles
  • Improves posture and flexibility
  • Opens the chest and lungs, improving breathing
  • Reduces stress and fatigue
  • Improves digestion by massaging abdominal organs
  • Helps relieve lower-back stiffness

Beginner Tips

Do not over-arch the back—lift only as much as comfortable.

Keep elbows slightly bent instead of forcing straight arms.

Engage core muscles to protect the lower back.

Practice on a soft yoga mat for better comfort.
