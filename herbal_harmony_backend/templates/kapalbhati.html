BALASANA (CHILD POSE)

Balasana, or Child's Pose, is a gentle resting pose that calms the mind and relieves tension in the body. It is often used as a resting position during yoga practice.

Sanskrit

Balasana (bah-LAHS-anna)

bala = child

Steps to Perform Balasana:

  1. Kneel on the floor with your big toes touching and knees spread apart to a comfortable width.
  2. Exhale and lower your torso between your thighs, extending your arms forward or resting them alongside your body.
  3. Rest your forehead on the mat and relax your entire body.
  4. Hold the pose for several breaths, allowing your body to sink deeper into relaxation.
  5. To release, gently lift your torso back up to a kneeling position.

Benefits of Balasana:

  • Stretches the hips, thighs, and ankles.
  • Calms the mind and relieves stress.
  • Alleviates back and neck pain when performed with proper alignment.
  • Improves circulation to the head and relieves fatigue.

Beginner Tips

Don’t be afraid to play with different iterations! You can spread your knees slightly wider or bring your arms alongside your legs, palms up. See what these modifications bring up for you and which version of the pose feels most connected to your body.

If you wish, instead of extending your arms on the floor, reach them back alongside your feet, palms facing up.

Don’t shy away from using blocks, blankets, or bolsters. Child’s Pose is meant to be restful and soothing. Whatever your best experience of the pose is, you should strive to create and lean into that posture.
