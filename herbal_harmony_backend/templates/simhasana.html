SIMHASANA (LION POSE)

Simhasana is a powerful seated posture that activates the throat chakra, releases stress, boosts confidence, and stimulates facial muscles. The roaring exhalation helps remove emotional tension and improves breathing.

Sanskrit Meaning

Simha = Lion

Asana = Pose

Meaning: “The Lion Pose”

Steps to Perform Simhasana:

  1. Kneel down and sit on your heels (Vajrasana position).
  2. Place your palms on your knees with fingers spread wide.
  3. Inhale deeply through your nose.
  4. Open your mouth wide and stretch your tongue out as far as possible.
  5. Exhale forcefully with a loud “Haa” sound like a lion’s roar.
  6. Open your eyes wide and look upward between the eyebrows.
  7. Repeat 5–7 rounds.

Benefits of Simhasana:

  • Reduces facial tension and strengthens facial muscles
  • Improves voice clarity and throat health
  • Releases anger, stress, and emotional blockages
  • Stimulates the throat and chest region
  • Boosts confidence and self-expression
  • Activates the respiratory system

Beginner Tips

Do not force the roar—keep it natural and relaxed.

If sitting on heels is uncomfortable, sit cross-legged.

Keep the spine straight and shoulders relaxed.

Focus on releasing stress with each exhalation.
