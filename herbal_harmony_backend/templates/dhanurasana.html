DHANURASANA (BOW POSE)

Dhanurasana is a powerful backbend that strengthens the spine, opens the chest, and improves flexibility. It helps stimulate the abdominal organs, improves posture, and boosts energy levels. It is one of the main backbending poses in traditional yoga.

Sanskrit Meaning

Dhanur = Bow

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Bow Pose”

Steps to Perform Dhanurasana:

  1. Lie on your stomach with legs hip-width apart and arms beside your body.
  2. Bend your knees and bring your heels close to your hips.
  3. Hold your ankles with both hands.
  4. Inhale and lift your chest, head, and thighs off the ground.
  5. Pull your legs upward to lift your upper body, forming a bow shape.
  6. Keep your gaze forward and breathe normally.
  7. Hold for 10–20 seconds, then exhale and release slowly.

Benefits of Dhanurasana:

  • Strengthens the back, spine, and core muscles
  • Improves posture and increases back flexibility
  • Opens the chest, shoulders, and hip flexors
  • Stimulates abdominal organs and improves digestion
  • Reduces stress, fatigue, and anxiety
  • Helps relieve mild back pain and stiffness

Beginner Tips

Do not over-strain your lower back—lift only as much as comfortable.

Keep knees hip-width apart; avoid bringing them too close.

Engage your core muscles to reduce pressure on the spine.

Practice slowly and avoid jerky movements.
