MARJARIASANA (CAT–COW POSE)

Marjariasana is a gentle spinal movement sequence that improves flexibility, relieves back pain, enhances breathing, and reduces stress. It is ideal for beginners and boosts overall spine health.

Sanskrit Meaning

Marjari = Cat

Asana = Pose or posture

Meaning: “The Cat Stretch Pose”

Steps to Perform Marjariasana:

  1. Start on your hands and knees in a tabletop position.
  2. Keep your wrists aligned under your shoulders and knees under hips.
  3. Inhale, drop your belly, arch your back, and look upward (Cow Pose).
  4. Exhale, round your spine upward, tuck your chin toward your chest (Cat Pose).
  5. Move smoothly between Cat and Cow with each breath.
  6. Repeat 10–15 rounds slowly.

Benefits of Marjariasana:

  • Improves flexibility of the spine
  • Relieves back, neck, and shoulder tension
  • Enhances digestion by massaging abdominal organs
  • Reduces stress and relaxes the mind
  • Improves posture and spinal alignment
  • Promotes better breathing and lung capacity

Beginner Tips

Keep movements slow and synchronized with breath.

Do not lock your elbows—keep them slightly soft.

Avoid forcing your spine; move gently.

Place a blanket under knees if floor is hard.
