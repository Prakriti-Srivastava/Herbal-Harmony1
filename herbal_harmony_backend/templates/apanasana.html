APANASANA (KNEES-TO-CHEST POSE)

Apanasana is a gentle reclining pose that relaxes the lower back, improves digestion, and releases gas and bloating. It also calms the nervous system and helps relieve stress and fatigue.

Sanskrit Meaning

Apana = Downward-moving life force

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Wind-Relieving Pose”

Steps to Perform Apanasana:

  1. Lie down on your back with legs extended.
  2. Bend both knees and bring them toward your chest.
  3. Hold your knees with your hands or interlock your fingers.
  4. Relax your shoulders and keep your spine on the floor.
  5. Gently pull your knees closer as you exhale.
  6. Breathe slowly and hold for 20–30 seconds.
  7. Optionally rock side-to-side for lower back massage.
  8. Release legs slowly and return to the starting position.

Benefits of Apanasana:

  • Relieves gas, bloating, and constipation
  • Improves digestion and stimulates abdominal organs
  • Reduces lower back pain and stiffness
  • Calms the mind and reduces stress
  • Gently massages the spine
  • Helps relax the hips and lower abdomen

Beginner Tips

Keep your neck and shoulders relaxed on the floor.

Do not lift your head while pulling knees in.

If you feel pressure in the knees, hold thighs instead.

Move slowly and avoid jerky movements.
