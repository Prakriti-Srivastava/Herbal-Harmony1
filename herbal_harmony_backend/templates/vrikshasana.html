VRIKSHASANA (TREE POSE)

Vrikshasana is a balancing posture that improves focus, stability, and concentration. It strengthens the legs, tones the hips, and helps develop mental clarity and calmness. It is a grounding pose that enhances mind-body coordination.

Sanskrit Meaning

Vriksha = Tree

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Tree Pose”

Steps to Perform Vrikshasana:

  1. Stand straight with feet together and arms by your sides.
  2. Shift your weight onto your left foot and lift your right foot.
  3. Place the right foot on your left inner thigh or calf (avoid the knee).
  4. Join your palms in Namaste and bring them in front of your chest.
  5. Once balanced, raise your arms overhead, keeping them straight.
  6. Keep your spine straight and gaze at a fixed point for balance.
  7. Hold for 10–20 seconds, then switch sides.

Benefits of Vrikshasana:

  • Improves balance and stability
  • Strengthens legs, ankles, and core muscles
  • Enhances concentration and mental calmness
  • Tones hips and thigh muscles
  • Improves posture and spinal alignment
  • Helps develop focus and confidence

Beginner Tips

Use a wall for support if you find it hard to balance.

Keep your gaze fixed on one still point to maintain stability.

Avoid placing the foot on the knee joint—use thigh or calf.

Engage your core muscles to stay steady.
