KAPALBHATI PRANAYAMA

Kapalbhati is a powerful breathing technique that involves forceful exhalation and passive inhalation. It helps detoxify the lungs, improves digestive fire, boosts metabolism, and enhances mental clarity.

Sanskrit

Kapalbhati (kah-pal-bhah-tee)

kapal = skull

bhati = shining

Meaning: “Skull-shining breath”

Steps to Perform Kapalbhati Pranayama :

  1. Sit in a comfortable meditative posture with your spine straight.
  2. Place your hands on your knees and relax your shoulders.
  3. Take a deep breath in normally.
  4. Exhale forcefully through your nose by pulling your stomach inward.
  5. Let inhalation happen automatically without effort.
  6. Continue this for 30–60 strokes per round, then relax and breathe normally.

Benefits of Kapalbhati:

  • Strengthens lungs and improves breathing capacity.
  • Boosts digestive system and metabolism.
  • Helps in weight loss by burning abdominal fat.
  • Enhances focus, clarity, and reduces stress.
  • Detoxifies the body by removing stale air from lungs.

Beginner Tips for Kapalbhati Pranayama

Start slowly with 15–20 strokes and increase gradually as your stamina improves.

Keep your chest still and let all the movement come from the abdomen.

If you feel dizzy, stop immediately and breathe normally.

Avoid Kapalbhati after meals; practice on an empty stomach for best results.
