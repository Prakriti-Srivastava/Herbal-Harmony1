RELAXED BREATHING SUPPORT

Relaxed Breathing Support is a gentle breathing and relaxation practice that may help the body feel calmer during cold, cough, mild congestion, and breathing discomfort. Soft breathing and supported posture help improve comfort without putting stress on the body.

Breathing Practice

Gentle Relaxation and Calm Breathing

Related practices: Deep Breathing, Relaxed Sitting, Gentle Chest Relaxation

Steps to Perform Relaxed Breathing Support:

  1. Sit comfortably on a chair or yoga mat with your back straight.
  2. Relax your shoulders and place your hands gently on your knees.
  3. Close your eyes and breathe slowly through your nose.
  4. Take a gentle deep breath in and slowly breathe out.
  5. Keep your breathing natural and avoid forceful inhaling or exhaling.
  6. Focus on relaxing the chest, neck, and shoulders.
  7. Continue calm breathing for 5–10 minutes.
  8. Slowly open your eyes and relax after the practice.

Benefits of Relaxed Breathing Support:

  • Helps calm the body and mind.
  • May support comfortable breathing during cold and cough.
  • Helps reduce chest tightness and stress.
  • Promotes relaxation and mental calmness.
  • Supports gentle respiratory comfort naturally.

Beginner Tips

Keep your breathing soft and natural. Do not force deep breathing.

Avoid strong pranayama during heavy congestion or severe cough.

Practice in a quiet and comfortable environment for better relaxation.

If you feel discomfort, dizziness, or breathing difficulty, stop immediately.

This practice is best for mild cold, cough, stress, and breathing discomfort.
