SUPTA BADDHA KONASANA
(RECLINING BOUND ANGLE POSE)

Supta Baddha Konasana is a deeply relaxing and restorative yoga pose. It gently opens the hips, relaxes the lower back, and calms the nervous system. This pose is commonly practiced for stress relief, flexibility, and mental relaxation.

Sanskrit Meaning

Supta = Reclining

Baddha = Bound

Kona = Angle

Meaning: “Reclining Bound Angle Pose”

Steps to Perform Supta Baddha Konasana:

  1. Lie down on your back with legs extended and arms at your sides.
  2. Bend your knees and bring the soles of your feet together.
  3. Let your knees drop outward naturally toward the mat, forming a diamond shape.
  4. Place your hands on your belly or beside your body, palms facing upward.
  5. Relax your shoulders, close your eyes, and soften your breath.
  6. Stay in the pose for 1–3 minutes, breathing deeply and allowing your body to relax.
  7. To release: gently lift the knees back together and roll to one side.

Benefits of Supta Baddha Konasana:

  • Relaxes the lower back
  • Improves hip flexibility
  • Reduces stress & anxiety
  • Improves blood circulation
  • Soothes digestive discomfort
  • Helps during menstrual discomfort
  • Calms the nervous system

Beginner Tips

Use cushions under your knees if hips feel tight.

Keep your spine neutral and relaxed.

Focus on soft, slow breathing.

Do not force the knees down—let gravity work.
