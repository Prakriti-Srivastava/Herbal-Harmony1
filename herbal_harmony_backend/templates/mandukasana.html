MANDUKASANA (FROG POSE)

Mandukasana is a powerful seated pose that stimulates abdominal organs, improves digestion, and helps reduce belly fat. It enhances hip flexibility, strengthens the back, and supports better posture.

Sanskrit Meaning

Manduka = Frog

Asana = Posture

Meaning: “The Frog Pose”

Steps to Perform Mandukasana:

  1. Sit in Vajrasana with your spine straight.
  2. Make fists with both hands, placing the thumbs inside the fingers.
  3. Place your fists on your lower abdomen on either side of the navel.
  4. Inhale deeply.
  5. Exhale and bend forward, applying gentle pressure on the abdomen.
  6. Keep your head, neck, and spine aligned.
  7. Hold the position for 10–20 seconds with relaxed breathing.
  8. Slowly return to the starting position.

Benefits of Mandukasana:

  • Improves digestion and reduces acidity
  • Helps manage diabetes by stimulating the pancreas
  • Reduces belly fat and tones the abdomen
  • Relieves gas, constipation, and bloating
  • Improves flexibility in hips and thighs
  • Strengthens lower back muscles

Beginner Tips

Avoid bending too deeply; go only as far as you are comfortable.

Keep fists firm to apply proper abdominal pressure.

If knees hurt in Vajrasana, place a soft cushion below.

Perform on an empty stomach for best results.
