SUKHASANA (EASY POSE)

Sukhasana is a comfortable seated posture that calms the mind, improves breathing, and prepares the body for meditation. It's ideal for beginners and useful for relieving mild back stiffness and stress.

Sanskrit

Sukhasana (SOO-kah-sah-nah)

sukha = ease, comfort

asana = posture

Meaning: “Comfortable / Easy Pose”

Steps to Perform Sukhasana:

  1. Sit on the floor with legs crossed comfortably (either simple cross or sit slightly elevated on a cushion).
  2. Lengthen your spine and relax your shoulders.
  3. Place hands gently on the knees (Gyan mudra optional).
  4. Close your eyes and take slow, deep breaths.
  5. Hold for 5–15 minutes as comfortable, focusing on steady breathing.

Benefits of Sukhasana:

  • Reduces stress and anxiety; calms the mind.
  • Improves focus and concentration.
  • Supports better posture and spinal alignment.
  • Helps with mild back stiffness and hip mobility.
  • Excellent for meditation and pranayama practice.

Beginner Tips for Sukhasana

If hips are tight, sit on a folded blanket or cushion to elevate the hips.

Keep weight distributed evenly on both sit bones and avoid slumping.

Use a wall for back support if you have trouble keeping the spine straight.

Start with short durations (3–5 minutes) and gradually increase.
