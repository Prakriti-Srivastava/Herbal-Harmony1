GENTLE NECK AND BREATHING PRACTICE

Gentle Neck and Breathing Practice is a simple relaxation yoga practice that combines slow neck movements with calm breathing. It may help reduce headache, neck stiffness, heaviness, and discomfort during mild cold.

Yoga Category

Yoga Relaxation and Breathing Practice

Related practices: Greeva Sanchalan, Deep Breathing, Relaxed Sitting

Steps to Perform Gentle Neck and Breathing Practice:

  1. Sit comfortably on a chair or yoga mat with your back straight.
  2. Relax your shoulders and keep your hands on your knees.
  3. Slowly inhale and gently move your neck upward.
  4. Exhale and slowly bring your neck downward.
  5. Now slowly turn your neck to the right side while breathing normally.
  6. Come back to the center and then turn your neck to the left side.
  7. After neck movement, close your eyes and take slow deep breaths for 2–3 minutes.
  8. Keep the practice gentle and do not force any movement.

Benefits of Gentle Neck and Breathing Practice:

  • Helps relax neck and shoulder muscles.
  • May reduce headache and heaviness.
  • Supports calm breathing during mild cold.
  • Helps reduce stress and body tension.
  • Improves relaxation and mental calmness.

Beginner Tips

Do not rotate your neck too fast. Keep all movements slow and comfortable.

If you feel pain, dizziness, or discomfort, stop the practice immediately.

Avoid forceful breathing when your nose is blocked or congestion is strong.

This practice is best for mild cold, headache, stress, and neck stiffness.

Practice for only 5–7 minutes in the beginning.
