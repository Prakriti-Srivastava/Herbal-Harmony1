PRANAYAMA (BREATHING EXERCISES)

Pranayama is the ancient yogic practice of controlling breath to energize, calm, and balance the body and mind. It improves lung capacity, reduces stress, and enhances focus and emotional stability.

Sanskrit Meaning

Prana = Life force / Breath

Ayama = Control / Expansion

Meaning: “Expanding and controlling the life force through breath”

Popular Pranayama Techniques:

1. Anulom Vilom (Alternate Nostril Breathing)

  1. Sit comfortably with a straight spine.
  2. Use right hand: close right nostril with thumb.
  3. Inhale slowly through the left nostril.
  4. Close left nostril with ring finger, open right.
  5. Exhale through the right nostril.
  6. Repeat by inhaling through right and exhaling through left.
  7. Continue for 5–10 rounds.

2. Bhramari Pranayama (Humming Bee Breath)

  1. Sit relaxed and close your eyes.
  2. Place fingers lightly on your ears.
  3. Inhale deeply through the nose.
  4. Exhale slowly while making a soft humming “mmm” sound.
  5. Repeat 5–7 times.

3. Kapalbhati (Skull-Shining Breath)

  1. Sit straight with hands on knees.
  2. Inhale normally.
  3. Exhale forcefully through the nose by pulling the stomach inward.
  4. Inhalation happens automatically.
  5. Perform 20–30 strokes, rest, then repeat.

4. Ujjayi Pranayama (Victorious Breath)

  1. Inhale through the nose while slightly constricting the throat.
  2. Produce a light ocean-like sound.
  3. Exhale slowly in the same manner.
  4. Continue for 5 minutes.

Benefits of Pranayama:

  • Boosts lung capacity & oxygen supply
  • Reduces stress, anxiety & mental tension
  • Improves concentration and memory
  • Enhances digestion & metabolism
  • Balances nervous system
  • Strengthens respiratory health
  • Improves emotional control
  • Promotes deep relaxation & better sleep

Beginner Tips

Start with gentle breathing—never force the breath.

Practice in a well-ventilated space.

Best done on an empty stomach.

Avoid fast pranayama if you feel dizzy.
