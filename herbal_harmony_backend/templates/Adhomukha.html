ADHO MUKHA SVANASANA (DOWNWARD-FACING DOG)

Adho Mukha Svanasana is a foundational yoga posture that energizes the body, stretches the spine and legs, strengthens the arms, and improves circulation. It is a rejuvenating pose often used as a transition or resting posture in yoga sequences.

Sanskrit

Adho Mukha Svanasana (AH-doh MOO-kah shvah-NAH-sah-nah)

adho = downward

mukha = face

svana = dog

asana = posture

Meaning: “Downward-Facing Dog Pose”

Steps to Perform Adho Mukha Svanasana:

  1. Start on your hands and knees in a tabletop position.
  2. Spread your fingers wide and press firmly into the ground.
  3. Tuck your toes under and lift your knees off the floor.
  4. Straighten your legs and raise your hips toward the ceiling, forming an inverted “V” shape.
  5. Keep your spine long, heels reaching toward the floor, and gaze toward your feet.
  6. Hold the pose for 5–10 breaths, then slowly release back to the starting position.

Benefits of Adho Mukha Svanasana:

  • Stretches the hamstrings, calves, and spine.
  • Strengthens the arms, shoulders, and wrists.
  • Improves blood flow to the brain, increasing energy.
  • Relieves back pain and enhances posture.
  • Calms the mind and reduces stress.

Beginner Tips

Bend your knees slightly if your hamstrings feel tight.

Keep your spine long—avoid rounding your back.

Press your hands firmly into the mat to relieve pressure from your wrists.

Pedal your feet (bend one knee at a time) to warm up the legs.
