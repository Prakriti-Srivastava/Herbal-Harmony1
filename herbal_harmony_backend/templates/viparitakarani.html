VIPARITA KARANI (LEGS-UP-THE-WALL POSE)

Viparita Karani is a deeply relaxing restorative posture where the legs are elevated to promote circulation, calm the nervous system, and reduce fatigue and stress. It is also helpful for digestion, swelling, and overall relaxation.

Sanskrit

Viparita Karani (vih-pah-REE-tah kah-RAH-nee)

viparita = inverted / reversed

karani = action

Meaning: “The Inverted Pose”

Steps to Perform Viparita Karani:

  1. Sit sideways next to a wall with your knees bent.
  2. Slowly lie back and swing your legs up the wall.
  3. Adjust your body so your hips are close to the wall and legs are straight upward.
  4. Relax your arms by your sides with palms facing upward.
  5. Close your eyes, breathe slowly, and stay in this pose for 5–15 minutes.

Benefits of Viparita Karani:

  • Reduces stress, anxiety, and nervous tension.
  • Improves blood circulation and reduces swelling in the legs.
  • Relieves lower back pain and relaxes the spine.
  • Helps with digestive issues and boosts relaxation.
  • Improves sleep quality and reduces fatigue.

Beginner Tips

Use a folded blanket under your hips for better comfort and support.

If your hamstrings feel tight, keep your hips slightly away from the wall.

Focus on slow and deep breathing to maximize relaxation.

Avoid doing this pose right after eating; keep at least 2 hours gap.
